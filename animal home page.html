Major Classifications of Animals based on their habitats are:

Terrestrial Animals

A Terrestrial Animal is an animal that primarily lives and thrives on land. These animals have adaptations that allow them to navigate and survive in terrestrial environments, such as forests, grasslands, deserts, and mountains.

Terrestrial animals have limbs or appendages suited for walking, running, digging, or climbing on solid ground. They have evolved a variety of adaptations to suit life on land, such as specialized limbs, respiratory systems, and skin coverings.

They have also evolved various adaptations to navigate and survive in terrestrial environments such as forests, grasslands, deserts, and mountains. Terrestrial animals can be categorized based on various criteria, including their characteristics, behaviors, and evolutionary relationships.

The place where Terrestrial Animals dwells is known as Terrestrial Habitat

Aquatic Animals

An Aquatic Animal is an animal that lives and is adapted to an aquatic or water-based habitat. These animals can be found in various aquatic environments such as oceans, rivers, lakes, and ponds. They have adaptations that enable them to swim, breathe underwater, and withstand buoyancy.

Aquatic animals can be further categorized as marine (ocean-dwelling) or freshwater (inhabiting rivers and lakes).

Aquatic Animals are dwellers of Aquatic Habitat

Amphibians

Amphibians are a group of vertebrate animals known for their ability to live both in Aquatic and Terrestrial environments during different stages of their life cycle. They typically begin their lives as aquatic larvae with gills and then undergo metamorphosis into adults that can live on land and breathe air.

Amphibians are characterized by their smooth, moist skin, and many have adaptations for both water and land habitats.

Since Amphibians can live in both Terrestrial and Aquatic habitat, they are categorized separately.

Although Amphibians can dwell in both Terrestrial and Aquatic Habitat, They have their own specific environments or habitats where amphibians live called Amphibian Habitat

Arboreal Animals

Arboreal animals are those that primarily live in trees or are adapted for life in tree-dwelling environments.

Arboreal animals have evolved various adaptations for climbing, perching, and moving through trees. These adaptations can include specialized limbs for gripping, prehensile tails for balance, and enhanced senses for detecting movement and predators in the canopy. Their unique adaptations that allow them to navigate, find food, and shelter within the complex three-dimensional environment of trees.

Arboreal Animals are dwellers of Arboreal Habitat

Aerial Animals

Aerial animals are organisms that are adapted to live and move within the airspace, or aerial habitat, above the surface of the Earth. These animals have evolved various adaptations that allow them to fly, glide, or float in the air. Aerial animals occupy the sky and have unique features that enable them to navigate this three-dimensional environment.

Aerial animals have evolved specific adaptations to achieve flight, which is a remarkable feat due to the challenges posed by air resistance, gravity, and the need for lift.

These adaptations may include:

  • Wings
  • Lightweight Skeletons
  • Muscular Strength
  • Feathers or Membranes

Aerial Animals are dwellers of Aerial Habitat

Note:
Though Most Aerial Animals may also be dwellers of other habitats, they spend most of their time in the air and why they are classified as Aerial Animals
