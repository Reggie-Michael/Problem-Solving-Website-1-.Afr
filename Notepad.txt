Forests: Many amphibian species inhabit forests, including both temperate and tropical rainforests. They can be found on the forest floor, in leaf litter, near streams, and on low vegetation.

Wetlands: Wetlands such as swamps, marshes, and ponds are important amphibian habitats. These areas provide breeding sites for many species due to the presence of water.
